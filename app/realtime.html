While the UK's rate of inflation hit its lowest level in three and a half years in April, to just 0.8 per cent, economists are warning that the effect of Covid-19 could push prices down further. Analysts from Ernst & Young's ITEM club, are expecting further falls in prices of consumer goods because of lower oil prices and weaker consumer activity. They say: > Inflation is likely to fall further and the EY ITEM Club believes it could get as low as 0.3% over the summer. > The near-term fundamentals for consumer spending appear to have taken a downturn as a result of coronavirus. Additionally, many incomes have been negatively affected. This is likely to keep consumers price conscious for some time. Stockbroker AJ Bell points out that the April inflation release was not the usual full list of price changes, because lockdown restrictions meant some services were unavailable to households. > The ONS had already acknowledged the problems of getting accurate pricing to calculate inflation and has given a list of the 90 items it’s impossible to add to its virtual shopping basket, which range from a pint of beer in the pub to child minder costs, the price of a manicure and theatre tickets. Renault earmarks three French factories for closure
雷诺指定关闭三家法国工厂-|-雷诺指定了三家法国工厂的关闭计划-|-雷诺指定关闭三家法国工厂
David Keohane in Paris
David Keohane 巴黎报道
Renault may close some of its French factories, part of a €2bn cost savings plan to be revealed later this month as the carmaker struggles with the impact of Covid-19 and falling demand. The group is considering closing three sites in France, including two smaller parts factories as well as Dieppe in the north of France, which produces the Alpine. A fourth larger factory at Flins could stay open but stop making cars within a few years. No decision has yet been taken. The discussions were first reported by French weekly newspaper Le Canard Enchaîné and confirmed by people familiar with the matter. The €2bn cost-cutting operation, which will take place over three years, was already planned before coronavirus and after Renault’s profits were nearly wiped out in 2019 — a year marked by turbulence following the arrest of the group's former boss, Carlos Ghosn. Renault is also close to signing a deal with banks for a €5bn loan backed by the French government. The agreement could be finalised as soon as Wednesday evening, say people familiar with the matter. Other companies such as airline Air France-KLM have secured similar deals. The car industry has been hit hard by the crisis. According to the French central bank, the French car industry last month used just 8 per cent of its potential production capacity. Most car makers have started to get their factories working again, after closing them in March, but are producing at well below normal capacity. Retail sales in Australia dropped at the fastest pace on record in April as lockdowns to limit the spread of Covid-19 hit restaurants and clothing sales. Retail turnover fell by a seasonally-adjusted 17.9 per cent in April compared to the previous month, according to preliminary figures from the Australian bureau of statistics. Retail sales had notched the strongest rise on record in March. In year-on-year terms, retail turnover fell 9.4 per cent in April. Falls for cafes, restaurants, clothing and department store sales were particularly pronounced, down by around half compared to a year earlier. “While March saw a mix of impacts related to Covid-19 across industries, these impacts were overwhelmingly negative in April, as regulations regarding social distancing measures limited the ability of businesses to trade as normal for the entire month,” ABS said. Supermarket sales, which had jumped in March as people stockpiled ahead of lockdowns fell month on month. Australia has begun relaxing its lockdown restrictions in recent weeks following success in reducing the spread of coronavirus.
